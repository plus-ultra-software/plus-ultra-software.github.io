What is a GUID?

A Globally Unique Identifier (GUID) or Universally Unique Identifier (UUID) is a 128-bit number used to identify information in computer systems. The term GUID generally refers to Microsoft's implementation of the UUID standard.

They are represented as 32 hexadecimal digits, displayed in five groups separated by hyphens, in the form 8-4-4-4-12 for a total of 36 characters (32 alphanumeric characters and 4 hyphens).

The total number of possible UUIDs is 2122, which means the probability of generating a duplicate UUID is essentially zero, making them ideal for unique database keys, tracking sessions, or transaction IDs without requiring a central authority to coordinate.
